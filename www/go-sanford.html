Go, Sanford!

It's good to finally see an airport questioning the insanity. I hope this becomes a trend. Unfortunately, they will still have to follow the TSA guidelines, but those change all the time and hopefully the private company will have higher standards of training and accountability for its employees.

The backlash continues over those new TSA screening measures, and now one Central Florida airport has decided to go with a private security screening firm. ... The TSA points out that even if an airport decides to use a private firm for security, the screeners still must follow TSA guidelines. That would include using enhanced pat-downs and the full-body scanners if they are installed at the airport.
